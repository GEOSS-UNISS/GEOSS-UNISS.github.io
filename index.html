Team

  • Prof. Erica Nocerino, Dr.

    Email: enocerino@uniss.it
    ResearchGate: Erica Nocerino

    Erica Nocerino is currently associate professor in geomatics at the University of Sassari, Department of Humanities and Social Sciences. Her research interests are in 3D digitization techniques, focusing on developing procedures and guidelines for creating 3D models of verified quality in various fields, such as cultural heritage, archaeology, biology, and medicine. In recent years, she has emphasized underwater photogrammetry, especially for autonomous navigation and image processing for temporal monitoring. As a scientific diver, she has participated in numerous underwater campaigns, including the Costa Concordia shipwreck and coral monitoring in French Polynesia. She chairs the ISPRS Working Group II/7 in Underwater Data Acquisition and Processing and leads projects such as ISPRS SI 2023 NAUTILUS and the Italian project MANATEE.

  • Fabio Menna, Dr.

    Email: fmenna@uniss.it

    Fabio Menna is a researcher at the University of Sassari. His main areas of expertise include photogrammetry, range sensors, geodetic surveying, and 3D modeling. As a scientific diver, he has extensive experience in underwater photogrammetry for engineering and industrial applications, including the Costa Concordia survey and the development of COMEX ORUS3D subsea photogrammetry systems. He is Cochair of the ISPRS WG II/7 on Underwater Data Acquisition and Processing, organizes scientific events on underwater photogrammetry, and leads the ISPRS ECBI 2024 POSER project.

  • Alessio Calantropio, Dr.

    Email: alessio@calantropio.it
    Website: calantropio.it

    Alessio Calantropio is a postdoctoral researcher in geomatics at the University of Sassari, Department of Humanities and Social Sciences. He graduated in Architecture from Politecnico di Torino with a focus on UAV applications for construction site safety and seismic emergency response. In 2023, he obtained a Ph.D. in Geomatics, specializing in the documentation of Underwater Cultural Heritage. As a scientific diver, he has significant expertise in underwater data processing, 2D and 3D marine mapping, and UAV applications for research and heritage monitoring. He actively contributes to ISPRS Working Group II/7 and the ISPRS SI 2023 NAUTILUS project.
